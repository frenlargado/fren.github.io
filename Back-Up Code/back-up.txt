<h1 class="our_story">OUR STORY</h1>
<div id="content">
<div class="paragraph_1">
	<p>Tabag's Place, like other places, was just an ordinary area until it became what it is now. Tabag's Place was previously an abandoned lot and grazing field for livestock such as pigs, horses, carabaos, and even poultry. The region was originally overgrown with tall grasses and plants, as well as various types of waste. But everything changed when the pandemic started.</p>
</div>

	<p>Cruzado and the Regis family chose to develop the land in the early months of the pandemic, which began in 2020, by intending to build a house. So, the area was cleaned, and another goal, which is to build the captivating Tabag Falls, was also accomplished. As the months passed, the area continued to grow, fueled by the determination to transform Tabag into something spectacular.</p>
</div>

	<p>After the houses were built and the growth of Tabag's Place continued, the family wanted to build a business, a tourist attraction, and a restaurant, which they did. The Tabag View deck was the first to emerge, providing tourists with a panoramic view of Mt. Malindig and the ocean to the east. Following the completion of the view deck, the crowning jewel of Tabag's Place was born, and people from all over the province came to enjoy the tasty food and beautiful scenery.</p>
